# WASTENOMORE / Amino — Brand Guidelines

## 1. Brand overview

**Brand / solution name:** WASTENOMORE  
**Supporting identity shown in concept board:** Amino  
**Context:** BCG Platinion Technology Consulting Challenge  
**Brand purpose:** A modern, trustworthy, sustainability-focused digital platform for waste management, recycling intelligence, and citizen engagement.

This brand should communicate:
- environmental responsibility
- operational intelligence
- public-sector trust
- simplicity and accessibility
- measurable impact

---

## 2. Brand essence

### Mission
Help regions, municipalities, operators, and citizens reduce waste and improve recycling through smart, connected, data-driven action.

### Vision
A cleaner, more efficient, and more circular region where waste systems are transparent, optimized, and easy for people to engage with.

### Brand promise
WASTENOMORE turns fragmented waste operations into clear, actionable intelligence.

### Core attributes
- trustworthy
- modern
- human-centered
- clean
- sustainable
- data-driven
- civic/public-service friendly

---

## 3. Brand personality

The visual direction suggests a brand that is:

- **Calm** — not loud or overly playful
- **Professional** — suitable for public institutions and operators
- **Accessible** — easy to understand for citizens
- **Responsible** — aligned with environmental goals
- **Confident** — clear, structured, and credible
- **Practical** — focused on real-world improvement, not abstract sustainability messaging

### Tone of voice
Use language that is:
- clear
- direct
- optimistic
- informative
- non-technical where possible
- evidence-based

Avoid language that feels:
- overly corporate
- preachy
- alarmist
- overly complex
- gimmicky

---

## 4. Brand naming structure

### Primary product name
**WASTENOMORE**

This works well as the main platform or solution name because it is:
- memorable
- mission-led
- strongly tied to the waste reduction challenge
- suitable for dashboards, app branding, and presentations

### Supporting name
**Amino**

This appears as the typographic identity in the concept image and can be used as:
- the parent design system name
- the project codename
- the internal product architecture / studio identity
- or removed from customer-facing contexts if WASTENOMORE is preferred as the sole market-facing name

### Recommended naming usage
For external-facing material:
- **WASTENOMORE** as the primary brand
- optionally use **by Amino** in small format if desired

Example:
- WASTENOMORE
- WASTENOMORE by Amino

For the POC and pitch deck, the cleanest option is:
- **WASTENOMORE** as the main solution brand

## 5. Logo guidance

## 5.1 Logo components
The concept board shows:
- a recycle-inspired symbol with a leaf integrated in the center
- the wordmark **WASTENOMORE**
- a secondary typographic concept using **Amino**

### Symbol meaning
The symbol communicates:
- recycling / circularity
- sustainability
- motion / continuous flow
- environmental care through the leaf motif

### Wordmark meaning
**WASTENOMORE** conveys:
- waste reduction
- action and urgency
- a practical sustainability mission

---

## 5.2 Recommended logo system

### Primary logo
- recycle/leaf icon
- WASTENOMORE wordmark

### Secondary logo
- icon only

### Tertiary / presentation lockup
- Amino wordmark
- optional WASTENOMORE lockup beneath or beside it

### Best practice
For the product UI, dashboards, and app:
- use **icon + WASTENOMORE**

For challenge presentation title slides:
- use **Amino + WASTENOMORE** only if you want to preserve both identities shown in the board

---

## 5.3 Logo usage principles
- Keep generous whitespace around the logo
- Prefer the green icon on light backgrounds
- Use monochrome black version only when color is unavailable
- Do not stretch, rotate, or recolor the logo outside the approved palette
- Do not place the primary logo on visually busy backgrounds without a container
- Keep icon and wordmark proportions consistent

## 7. Typography

The concept board references:

### Headline font
**Amino**  
Use as a display or presentation font for:
- cover slides
- big headings
- hero sections
- major visual branding moments

### Body / UI font
**Open Sans**  
Use for:
- dashboard UI
- app UI
- body text
- charts and labels
- tables
- explanatory copy

---

## 7.1 Typography recommendations

### For presentations
- Title / section headers: Amino
- Body text / charts / labels: Open Sans

### For product UI
- Use Open Sans across the interface for consistency and readability
- Reserve Amino for logo or rare top-level branded headings only

---

## 7.2 Type hierarchy suggestion

### H1
- Amino or Open Sans Bold
- large, clean, high whitespace

### H2
- Open Sans Bold / Semibold

### H3
- Open Sans Semibold

### Body
- Open Sans Regular

### Caption / metadata
- Open Sans Regular or Semibold at smaller sizes

---

## 7.3 Typography principles
- Keep hierarchy obvious
- Use generous spacing
- Avoid overly dense text blocks
- Prefer sentence case over all caps except for the WASTENOMORE wordmark
- Keep dashboards highly legible

---

## 8. Visual style

## 8.1 Overall style direction
The identity is:
- minimal
- structured
- spacious
- eco-modern
- information-friendly

### The design should feel:
- clean rather than decorative
- professional rather than playful
- calm rather than aggressive
- modern rather than bureaucratic

---

## 8.2 Shapes and UI language
Recommended UI style:
- rounded corners, but subtle
- simple cards
- lots of whitespace
- light backgrounds
- restrained use of green for emphasis
- clean maps and charts
- iconography with rounded/simple geometry

### Avoid
- heavy gradients
- neon greens
- cluttered dashboards
- over-illustration
- overly dark interfaces
- too many competing accent colors

---

## 8.3 Imagery style
Use imagery that reflects:
- recycling infrastructure
- communities
- waste collection operations
- clean urban environments
- simple eco-technology

Preferred image treatment:
- bright
- natural
- realistic
- low-saturation where needed
- consistent with public-sector credibility

## 11. Presentation / pitch deck branding

For the BCG challenge deck, apply the brand as follows:

### Cover slide
- large Amino or WASTENOMORE mark
- recycle/leaf icon
- light background `#f3f3f3`
- subtle divider line in sage or green
- minimal text

### Section slides
- use large clean titles
- keep one strong brand color accent
- apply wide margins and whitespace

### Content slides
- Open Sans for all body content
- limited palette
- simple diagrams
- green for emphasis and key proof points
- avoid visual clutter

### Demo slides
- show product screens framed in the brand system
- use consistent iconography
- keep environmental theme subtle and professional

---

## 12. UI component style guidance

### Buttons
Primary:
- fill: `#267351`
- text: white

Secondary:
- outline or soft fill with `#305c54` / `#c0d3c7`

### Cards
- white or very light sage backgrounds
- soft shadow or thin border
- moderate padding
- clean title hierarchy

### Tags / chips
- sage background for neutral informational tags
- dark green for active/selected state

### Charts
- keep palette restrained
- use primary green and dark teal-green first
- use sage for secondary areas
- use black/grey sparingly for structure

### Maps
- use light neutral base
- overlay municipality or hotspot layers in brand greens
- ensure readability and contrast

---

## 13. Accessibility guidance

The brand should remain highly legible and inclusive.

Requirements:
- sufficient contrast for text and controls
- avoid relying on color alone for status
- keep charts readable with labels/tooltips
- use clear hierarchy and spacing
- support accessible font sizes in product UI

Because the palette is soft, ensure:
- darker greens are used for text and actionable elements
- sage is not used for small critical text
- black or dark green is used for body copy

---

## 14. Do / don't guidelines

## Do
- use lots of whitespace
- keep layouts calm and structured
- use the green palette consistently
- make dashboards readable first, branded second
- use Open Sans heavily in UI
- reserve Amino for branding moments
- keep sustainability cues subtle and credible

## Don't
- overload screens with too many shades
- make the product feel childish or overly gamified
- use bright saturated eco-colors outside the palette
- use decorative environmental graphics excessively
- mix too many fonts
- sacrifice clarity for branding
